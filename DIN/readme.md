**Abstract:**


This research paper delves into the dual-interpretation nature of some news events-moments where headlines trigger conflicting market signals – and how this market condition drives price discovery. EMH (Fama, 1970) Suggests that information is ‘priced in’ instantly. However, real-world markets are rarely that clear. By Analyzing over thousands news events and categorizing them based on market participants’ reaction and behavior, I have mentioned few news headlines that carries ambiguous, multi-layered narrative. I will use one news event covering the Recessionary pivot narrative after the August 4th 2024 Nonfarm payroll release. 

My findings suggest that purely quantitative models often fail during these regime shifts because they lack situational awareness. To solve this, I propose a "Dynamic Execution" framework—a hybrid approach that blends context-heavy programming with discretionary human oversight.  Ultimately, the data shows that in today’s markets, the context surrounding a headline is just as vital for valuation as the news itself.


**Quant Models Used:**
VAR, GARCH
